# Jungian Shadow Basics (Mock)

The shadow refers to traits, emotions, and impulses a person does not like to identify as "me."
These can include envy, anger, shame, dependency, and a wish for control.

When hidden material is denied, it often appears indirectly:

- overreaction to specific people
- compulsive criticism
- moral superiority
- emotional numbness followed by sudden intensity

Working with shadow is not self-blame.
It is the practice of recognizing disowned parts and integrating them into conscious life with responsibility.

Possible journaling indicators:

- "I hate that they are needy" with strong emotional charge
- "I am never angry" while body language suggests tension
- repeating relationship conflict with similar roles
